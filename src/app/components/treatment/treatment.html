Treatment: How to Fix Trigger Points

Trigger points don’t disappear on their own. Muscles won't relax from stretching, rest, or pills. The real solution is targeted physical pressure.

The Only Real Treatment: Myopressure

The only proven way to treat trigger points and restore muscle health is myopressure — a deep, firm, sometimes intense pressure technique aimed at physically breaking down trigger points and fibrosis.

Trigger points are hardened knots in muscle fibers. They don’t dissolve with gentle massage, pills, ointments, or exercises — they must be mechanically destroyed. Fibrosis often develops around long-lasting trigger points, making the tissue even tougher, denser, and harder to release without proper deep pressure.

Key Principles

  • Identify the right muscle: pain is usually referred, so the painful area is rarely the source.
  • Find the trigger point: learn to palpate the muscle and detect small, painful nodules.
  • Recognize fibrosis: long-term damage feels rough, stiff, and rope-like.
  • Treatment therapy: myopressure — a firm, targeted deep-pressure technique used to physically break down trigger points and fibrosis.
  • After treatment: warm the muscle with a hot, damp towel, in the bath, or in the shower, and stretch gently.
  • Treatment frequency: every 4–6 days, once the muscle has fully recovered from the previous myopressure.

What Does Not Work
And Makes Sore Muscles Worse

Any type of exercise — static or dynamic, heavy or light — loads the muscles and aggravates trigger points. Muscles need relaxation and restoration of length, not work. Swimming does not treat triggers. Running does not treat them. Yoga does not treat them. Strength training does not heal the muscles.

Stretching cold, unprepared muscles only worsens trigger points. You cannot stretch out a trigger point — you will only damage the muscle further.

Classical massage does not reach the deeper muscle layers where trigger points form. At best, it temporarily improves blood flow and reduces pain during the session. At worst, it irritates the trigger points even more.

No pill, injection, or ointment heals muscles or removes trigger points. They only mask symptoms, dull the pain, or temporarily shift active trigger points into a latent state.

Heating relaxes muscles, boosts circulation, and temporarily reduces pain — but it does not treat trigger points. Heat is useful only after proper myopressure treatment.

Dry needling or acupuncture does not heal muscles and does not remove trigger points. It may provide temporary relief or shift a fresh trigger into a latent state — but only if it is shallow, easy to reach, and not complicated by fibrosis.

Cold exposure aggravates trigger points. Cold is harmful for muscles.

Stress creates new trigger points and worsens existing ones.

Herniated discs do not cause pain and do not pinch nerves. Menisci do not hurt. Bones and cartilage do not feel pain. Muscles affected by trigger points are what hurt. Spinal and joint surgeries do not treat muscles and do not remove trigger points.
