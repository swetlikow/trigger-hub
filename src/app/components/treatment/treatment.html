Treatment: How to Fix Trigger Points

Trigger points don’t disappear on their own. Muscles won't relax from stretching, rest, or pills. The real solution is targeted physical pressure.

The Only Real Treatment: Myopressure

The only proven way to treat trigger points and restore muscle health is myopressure — a deep, firm, sometimes intense pressure technique aimed at physically breaking down trigger points and fibrosis.

Trigger points are hardened knots in muscle fibers. They don’t dissolve with gentle massage, pills, ointments, or exercises. They must be mechanically destroyed.

Key Principles

  • Find the trigger point: learn to palpate the muscle and detect small, painful nodules.
  • Recognize fibrosis: long-term damage feels rough, stiff, and rope-like.
  • Identify the right muscle: pain is usually referred, so the painful area is rarely the source.
  • Treatment frequency: mild points daily, severe ones every 2–3 days.
  • After treatment: warm the muscle, stretch gently, and move slowly.

What Does Not Work

These methods do not heal muscles and should be avoided completely:

❌ Any kind of exercise
❌ Physical activity (gym, sports, hard training)
❌ Hypothermia (cold exposure tightens muscles)
❌ Stress (creates new trigger points)
❌ Ointments, pills, injections — none repair muscle fibers
❌ Stretching cold or damaged muscles — increases tearing

A muscle must be warmed, pressed, and released before any stretching.
